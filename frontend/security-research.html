Vulnerability Disclosure Policy

ShieldIQ supports responsible disclosure. We ask that researchers follow the guidelines below to protect our users while giving us time to investigate and resolve confirmed issues.

✅ Authorized Testing

  • Testing against accounts you own or have explicit permission to test
  • Automated scanning against your own account with rate limiting respected
  • Reviewing publicly accessible endpoints and responses
  • Static analysis of our browser extension

🚫 Prohibited Activities

  • Accessing, modifying, or deleting data belonging to other users
  • Denial of service attacks or intentional service degradation
  • Social engineering of ShieldIQ staff or customers
  • Physical attacks or physical access attempts
  • Publishing vulnerability details before we've had time to resolve them

📋 Responsible Disclosure Expectations

  • Give us reasonable time to investigate and remediate before public disclosure
  • Provide sufficient detail to reproduce the issue
  • Do not exploit the vulnerability beyond what's needed to demonstrate it
  • Notify us promptly if you accidentally access data you shouldn't have
Safe Harbor
If you conduct security research in good faith and follow this policy, ShieldIQ will not pursue legal action against you. We consider good-faith security research to be authorized, beneficial activity and will work with you to understand and resolve any issues discovered. We ask that you give us a reasonable opportunity to address vulnerabilities before any public disclosure.
